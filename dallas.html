City Overview

Dallas is the fourth-most populous metropolitan area in the United States at 7.5 million people. Dallas was formally incorporated as a city on February 2, 1856. Today, Dallas is a vibrant and diverse urban city known for its world-class museums, thriving arts scene, and famous sports teams.

(Source: Wikipedia)

Region

Dallas lies along the Trinity River near the junction of the river’s three forks. Its winters are mild with brief cold spells, but summers are hot with moderate to high humidity. Dallas is the state’s third most populous city (after Houston and San Antonio) and the metropolis of the sprawling Dallas–Fort Worth urban area, known locally as the Metroplex.

(Source: Britannica)

Average Income Level and Economy

Average income in Dallas is $70,852 with about a 66.5% employment rate. Dominant sectors of its diverse economy include defense, financial services, information technology, telecommunications, and transportation. The construction of the Interstate Highway System reinforced Dallas's prominence as a transportation hub

(Source: US Census)
